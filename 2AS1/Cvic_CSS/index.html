Marie Terezie

Introduction

Marie Terezie (Marie Theresa) was the only female ruler of the Habsburg dominions and the last of the House of Habsburg.

Early Life

Marie Terezie was born on May 13, 1717, in Vienna, Austria, as the eldest daughter of Charles VI, Holy Roman Emperor.

Rule and Achievements

Marie Terezie became the Holy Roman Empress after her father's death in 1740. She implemented numerous reforms in her dominions, strengthening the Habsburg monarchy and modernizing its administration.

Legacy

Her reign had a lasting impact on Central Europe, and she is often remembered for her significant contributions to her empire's prosperity.

Selected Achievements

  • Centralized the administration of the Habsburg dominions.
  • Reformed the legal system.
  • Supported education and the arts.

Family

  1. Husband: Francis I, Holy Roman Emperor
  2. Children:
    • Maria Theresa of Austria
    • Joseph II, Holy Roman Emperor
    • Leopold II, Holy Roman Emperor
